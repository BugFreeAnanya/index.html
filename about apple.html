ABOUT APPLE

Apples are a healthy fruit, a good source of fiber, vitamin C, and antioxidants like quercetin. They come in many varieties and colors, can be eaten raw or used in cooking, and are associated with a reduced risk of conditions like heart disease and type 2 diabetes. While the whole fruit is most beneficial, apples are also used for juices, cider, and other products.It is a round, edible fruit (Malus domestica) with a fleshy, edible part that grows from the flower's base, and it has a firm core containing seeds. Apples are known for being rich in carbohydrates.

WHAT IS SO SPECIAL ABOUT APPLE?

Apples were most consistently associated with reduced risk of cancer, heart disease, asthma, and type II diabetes when compared to other fruits and vegetables and other sources of flavonoids. Apple consumption was also positively associated with increased lung function and increased weight loss.
