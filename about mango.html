ABOUT MANGO

The mango, or Mangifera indica, is a sweet, juicy tropical drupe (stone fruit) native to South Asia, known as the "king of fruits," packed with vitamins (A, C, B6), minerals, fiber, and antioxidants, offering benefits for skin, digestion, and immunity, and used fresh, in drinks, or cooked in various dishes. With hundreds of varieties and distinct colors, shapes, and flavors, it's a staple in tropical diets and a major crop in India and Southeast Asia, enjoyed globally for its rich taste and nutritional profile.

WHAT IS SO SPECIAL ABOUT MANGO FRUIT?

Mangoes are special for their nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins A and C, and a wide range of antioxidants. They also offer various health benefits, such as boosting immunity, supporting digestive and eye health, and promoting healthy skin. Culturally, mangoes are known as the "king of fruits," are a global favorite, and are used in numerous culinary products.
